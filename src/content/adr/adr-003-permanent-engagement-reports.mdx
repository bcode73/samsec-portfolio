## Context

Every SamSec Agent engagement executes real commands against real infrastructure, with reasoning, risk
classifications, and outcomes attached to each step.

## Problem

Without a durable record, there's no way to reconstruct why an autonomous engagement did what it did after the
fact. That matters for the operator's own understanding of what happened, and even more during incident review
when something has gone wrong and the reasoning behind a past action needs to be checked.

## Decision

Engagement Reports are stored permanently, not as ephemeral session logs, as a searchable record of
objectives, commands, output, timing, reasoning, and decisions for every engagement, with sensitive data
automatically masked before storage.

## Alternatives Considered

- **Ephemeral logs cleared at the end of each session**: rejected. This destroys the audit trail at exactly
  the moment it would be needed, after the fact, when something needs to be understood or investigated.
- **Opt-in logging**: rejected. An audit trail that exists for some engagements and not others isn't reliable
  enough to trust during an investigation.
- **Storing full raw command output without masking**: rejected. Command output can contain credentials or
  other sensitive data, and permanent storage without masking would turn a safety feature into a new exposure
  risk.

## Trade-offs

Permanent storage means the product carries an ongoing data-retention responsibility instead of treating logs
as disposable. Automatic masking of sensitive data adds real engineering complexity, but it's what makes
permanent retention safe to commit to in the first place.

## Consequences

Operators can replay engagement history and search past engagements rather than relying on memory. This is the
concrete implementation of "operational work is documented," one of SamSec Ops's core engineering principles.
It also means the masking logic needs ongoing security review as new command types and integrations are
added, since a gap there would undermine the whole guarantee.

## Related Decisions

Builds directly on [ADR-002: Human Authorization Before Autonomous Execution](/adr/adr-002-human-authorization):
the attestations and confirmations required by that decision are exactly what gets captured here.

## Future Revisions

If cross-platform support introduces engagements initiated outside of SamSec Ops itself, for example through a
future API, this decision will need to specify how those engagements are captured under the same retention and
masking model.
